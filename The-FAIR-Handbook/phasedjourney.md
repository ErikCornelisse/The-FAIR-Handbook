# Why a Phased Journey?

</br>

A **Phased Journey** is used as a "logic layer" to explain that implementing a collaborative ecosystem for data-sharing is a strategic evolution rather than a single technical event. This framework is essential for four key reasons:

- **Cognitive Load Management:** It prevents "analysis paralysis" by breaking complex legal, technical, and ethical requirements into manageable stages, allowing stakeholders to focus on foundational decisions before technical specifics.

- **Strategic Alignment:** It prioritizes the "why" before the "how," ensuring that leadership agrees on value propositions and ethical guardrails before technical teams begin implementation.

- **Progressive Trust Building:** In the sensitive healthcare domain, it creates a "trust ladder" where participants start with low-risk collaboration and move toward live data sharing only after mutual trust is codified.

- **Regulatory and Financial Gatekeeping:** It establishes "quality gates" that allow organizations to manage costs and ensure EHDS compliance at every step, providing clear opportunities to pivot or validate the project before committing significant resources.

## The Five Phases

The following phased journey outlines the strategic evolution of a healthcare data space, utilizing a **RACI** matrix — an assignment model that identifies who is **R**esponsible for the work, **A**ccountable for the outcome, **C**onsulted for expertise, and **I**nformed of progress — to ensure clear governance at every stage. The stakeholder roles and their RACI assignments are described in [Roles & Stakeholders](roles.md).

1. **Phase 1: Exploration & Value** — Defining the clinical use case and business model.

2. **Phase 2: Governance & Ethics** — Establishing the "Rules of the Game" and patient opt-out flows.

3. **Phase 3: Legal Foundation** — Signing the Rulebook and securing HDAB permits.

4. **Phase 4: Technical Integration** — Deploying connectors and Secure Processing Environments (SPE).

5. **Phase 5: Operational Excellence** — Monitoring data quality and clinical outcomes.

## Why Not a Thematic Reference?

Traditional handbooks organise content by topic (legal, technical, governance) and expect the reader to synthesise the connections themselves. The phased approach instead sequences content along the natural decision flow of a real project:

- Each phase has a clear entry criterion (what must be true before you start) and a clear exit criterion (what must be true before you move on).
- Stakeholders can immediately locate *where they are* in the journey and focus only on what is relevant now.
- Governance, legal and technical concerns are introduced at the moment they become actionable — not all at once.

This reduces risk by ensuring that organisations do not skip foundational decisions (such as establishing governance or signing legal agreements) in their eagerness to reach the technical integration phase.
